Experience

Here's some of the work I've done.


Salesforce Developer | IPIRA

In October of 2016, I started working as a part-time Salesforce Developer and Administrator at Intellectual Property and Industry Research Alliances (IPIRA), which is a department that manages intellectual property data, copyrights, and patents for inventions and research in Berkeley.

Over the past few months, I have developed and deployed (with the help of two other developers) an online Invention Disclosure Portal, which allows inventors to submit a web-based disclosure form and automatically loads information and data into our Salesforce org. This involved over a thousand lines of code on both the front end and back end, utilization of Salesforce's programming languages (e.g. Apex and Visualforce markup) and APIs, and administration and ETL of over 12,000 user records on the Salesforce platform.

New features I have developed include:
- Shared-access privileges (allowing multiple inventors to fill out a single disclosure)
- An improved user interface, written with Salesforce’s Visualforce markup language (similar to HTML) and static CSS styling resources
- Back-end Apex controllers that triggers workflows within the Intellectual Property department
- Automatic PDF generation
- Automated sending of custom emails (using a controller that links inventor info to contacts in the UC Berkeley directory)

I am currently working on an online Software Disclosure Form specifically for developers seeking licensing and patents for their software products.


User-Acceptance Tester - Intern | SFDT

From June to August of 2015, I interned at the San Francisco Department of Technology, where I worked with the development/project management team of the SF Business Portal. The SF Business Portal is an online government digital service that helps local users start businesses by providing a simple yet comprehensive step-by-step guide, as well as all the permits, licensing, and information that new business owners needs to start and grow in the city.

I edited and tested front-end site content and design, and I also consolidated business permit data from over 26 San Francisco city department in order to make the process of starting a business streamlined, intuitive, and custom-tailored to each specific type of business.

On the non-technical side, this involved a LOT of flowcharts, spreadsheets, phone calls, visits to city departments, and even a personal visit to mayor Ed Lee's main office. I loved the opportunity to participate in the many interactions between the development teams of different city departments, between private sector tech companies (such as Salesforce and Google) and government departments, and between locals businesses and the City and Country of San Francisco.
